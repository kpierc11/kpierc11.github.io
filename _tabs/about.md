Hi all,

My name is Kaleb Pierce, and I've been a professional web developer for the past five years. I've worked with several web platforms, such as WordPress, as well as many programming languages and frameworks.

The reason I started programming, as I'm sure most people say, was to develop video games. I was previously a trumpet player and planned to become a music teacher or a professional musician. After sitting in one of the practice rooms following a long practice session, I realized for the first time that this wasn't what I should be doing. I didn't enjoy it anymore. The pressure and anxiety of constant performances and long practice hours had sapped a lot of my passion for pursing music as a career. I knew I needed to pursue a different path.

I took some time to self-reflect and asked myself, "What do I spend most of my time doing?" Playing games, of course! I thought that since I enjoyed playing them, talking about them, and diving deep into their lore and stories, I would love making them. I started buying books and watching introductory game development videos, while getting my associates degree.

Trying to learn game development proved to be extremely difficult. I made some progress and dabbled in several game engines, but I still haven't completed a full game that I'm satisfied with. I still have unfinished game projects in Godot, Unreal Engine, and SDL3. I decided to switch gears for a few years and focus on a career in web development. Game development jobs are extremely competitive, and there are many talented individuals in the industry. So here I am now a few years into my web development career and I'm ready to go fully into game development. Along with making games I'm also interested in emulators and game engines.
